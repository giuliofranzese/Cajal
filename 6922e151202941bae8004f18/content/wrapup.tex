\section{Conclusions}
\begin{frame}{In summary}
\begin{itemize}
    \item In this talk we discussed about a research program rooted in the exploitation of Markov Processes to estimate "\textit{information metrics}".
    \item Starting from diffusion processes in Euclidean spaces, we generalised results to discrete (categorical) spaces and gave hints about infinite dimensional ones.
    \item We discussed interesting extensions of the proposed methodology to compute information decompositions across: i) elements of a system ii) the time axis.
\end{itemize}
What we left out (we have some backup slides)
\begin{itemize}
\item We made it \textit{look} simple ("just adapt these formulas and you get an estimator")
    \item A more formal discussion of the results
    \item Actual extensive numerical validation of the proposed methods
    \item Careful discussion of the convergence properties in terms of samples sizes, model complexity...
\end{itemize}
    
\end{frame}
